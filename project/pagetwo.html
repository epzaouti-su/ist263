2018-2019 Premier League Winners

Key Contributors:

Vincent Kompany: this 6’3” Belgian center back was 32 years old during the 2018-2019 season. He was the team captain and was consistently viewed the team leader and a sort of ‘rock’ in the back line.

Sergio Aguero: this 5’9” Argentinian striker was 30 years old during the 2018-2019 season. He was the team’s starting striker and had 16 league goals during the season. He acted as the team’s clinical striker that could be counted on for clutch plays.

Raheem Sterling: this 5’7” English winger was only 24 years old during the 2018-2019 season. He was an extremely fast and greatly influential winger that was top 5 in the league in goals, ending the season with a total of 20 league goals. This was a very defining season in the rising star's career, as it served to put him on the map as one of the best wingers in the league.

Kevin De Bruyne: this 5’11” Blegian central midfielder was 27 years old during the 2018-2019 season. He is highly regarded as one of the greatest midfielders in the world because of his amazing passing and creative ability. He finished the 2018-2019 season with 11 assists, which was the second most players assists in the league.

Manchester City won the EPL two seasons ago during the 2018-2019 season. This was the clubs 4th time winning the EPL, and most fans will say that this was their most impressive season. Their Spanish manager, Pep Guardiola, led a team that was riddled with injury to a total of 98 EPL points and record of 32 wins, 2 draws, and 4 losses. The ex-Barcelona legend had to strategically manage a squad of stars to the impressive league record. His style of play is one that combines fast ‘tiki-taka’ passing football with possession, calculated attacks, and intense defensive pressure. This style of play calls for constant movement and a large amount of squid stamina. The average midfielder ran a total of ~13km or ~8 miles over the 90 minute game. This taxing, but effective style of play is what allowed Manchester City to win the league.
